## How to Read This Guide

Each config gets a **Buy Signal Strength** and a **Maximum Buy Price (MBP)** — the ceiling price where the deal still qualifies as "high confidence."

**Signal Strength Scale:**

```
KKKK  95%+   No-brainer. Buy immediately.
KKK   85%+   Strong buy. Very good deal.
KK    70%+   Moderate buy. Fair price.
K     50%+   Weak buy. Only if you need it.
✗     <50%   Wait. Not a good deal in current climate.
```

**Threshold Logic:**
- MBP is calculated as: `Original MSRP × (1 - Depreciation Factor) × (1 + DRAM Premium)`
- DRAM Premium = +15–25% for 2026 (still rising), +0% for plateau, −10% for decline
- Higher RAM SKUs get HIGHER buy confidence because DRAM shortage hits them hardest

## 6️⃣ The Golden Rules for 2026

### Rule 1: High RAM > New Chip

```
M3 Max 128GB  >  M4 Max 64GB  >  M5 Max 36GB
     ↑                  ↑               ↑
   KKKKK              KKKK              K
```

**128GB of unified memory is worth more than chip generation** for any serious ML/AI/data work. The M3 Max is still a beast — it's the RAM that bottlenecks LLMs, not the chip.

### Rule 2: Never Buy New Apple Right Now

The June 25, 2026 price hike means new Macs are +18–33% more expensive than they were last week. The **used market hasn't fully adjusted yet** — there's a 2–4 week lag before used prices catch up. **This is the golden window.**

### Rule 5: The Japan Premium

In Japan, Macs carry an additional +10–20% premium due to:
- Weak yen (imported goods are expensive)
- Apple Japan pricing already higher than US
- Japanese market prefers higher-spec configs (culture of buying the best)

**What this means:** If you find a Japanese-market Mac at or below US used prices, **that's automatically a stronger buy signal.** The ¥476k M3 Max 128GB deal is a perfect example — in the US that same config sells for ~$3,000–3,200, but in Japan it's a steal at ¥476k (~$3,100).
