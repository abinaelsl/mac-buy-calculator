## 1️⃣ Background: The DRAM Crisis

### What Caused This?

The 2025–present global memory shortage is fundamentally different from prior cycles:

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **HBM cannibalization** | DDR5/HBM2E/HBM3 share the same wafer node. One HBM chip consumes ~3× the wafer area of DDR5. Manufacturers shifted capacity to HBM (10× more profitable per wafer for AI). |
| **AI hyperscaler demand** | Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta placed open-ended orders — "accept as much supply as available regardless of cost" (Reuters). |
| **OpenAI Stargate** | Requires ~900,000 wafers/month — ~40% of global DRAM output. Strategic partnership with Samsung and SK Hynix for undiced wafers. |
| **Supply constraints** | Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron all prioritizing HBM. Micron exited its consumer 'Crucial' brand entirely. |

### Price Impact (Mid-2025 → Present)

| Component | Mid-2025 Low | Current (Jul 2026) | Increase |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| DDR5 32GB (2×16GB) | $80–120 | $300–500 | ~300–400% |
| DDR4 32GB (2×16GB) | $55–70 | $250–350 | ~400–500% |
| DDR5 64GB (2×32GB) | $150–200 | $600–900+ | ~300–500% |

Sources: [IQON Digital], [Tom's Hardware], [TrendForce]

**DRAM contract prices surged 172% in 2025 alone** and all signs point to continued increases through 2026.
